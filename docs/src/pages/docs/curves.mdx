# Curve Catalog

Browse the built-in curves. Each curve has unique geometry, period, and behavior. Click any curve to see the details of its equation, parameters, and a live preview.

## Available Curves

### Standard

Some of the well-known and well-documented curves with easy to find mathematical definitions.

<Alert variant="warning">Their commonality does not take away from their inherent beauty. So, please try to enjoy each curve *equally*, and <u>do not</u> show preference for any over the others.</Alert>

### Iconic

These are mainly **tributes** to the impactful happenings of nature, humanity and univcerse. A recreation of shapes from the real world, whose <span class='text-muted hover:text-foreground duration-300 transition-all [font-variation-settings:"wght"_var(--wght)] [--wght:300] hover:[--wght:650] cursor-vertical-text'>silhouttes</span> carry a deeper meaning beyond their mathematical equations.

### Artemis II
<div class="inline-flex gap-4 items-center text-sm mt-1 mb-2">
  <Link href="/docs/curves/artemis2/"><Badge text="NASA" /></Link>

As the *first* built-in curve of the Sarmal library, this is my <span class="text-muted">amateur</span> attempt at drawing the Artemis II mission trajectory as a Catmull-Rom spline using <Link href="/play/">Playground</Link>. I attempted to trace  the outline of the lunar free-return trajectory by referencing [Artemis II Flight Path Animations](https://svs.gsfc.nasa.gov/20412/) from NASA.
